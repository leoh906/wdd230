Visual Hierarchy

BBC News

bbc.com/news

The BBC's homepage shows visual hierarchy by changing the sizes of its different fonts. In the example picture we can see a strong difference between the main article the site wants to show and the smaller articles. The smaller articles take up less space on the page. In addition there is contrast between the heading "news" and the smaller links leading to subtopics of the news page.

Fitt's Law

Church of Jesus Christ Website

churchofjesuschrist.org

On the homepage, there is a layer of pictures that are tightly packed together. Fitt's law argues that items packed closer together makes it easier for the user to access different elements. This tight grouping of pictures helps the user see and click on pictures with less effort engaged in scrolling.

Repetition

Wikipedia

Wikipedia.com

Wikipedia's pages are good examples of repetition. Although it's layout has rather bland colors and nearly identical layouts on all sites, this becomes an advantage for users who want to quickly scroll through wikipedia articles. The use of repetition makes it faster for people to read through wikipedia's numerous articles because they become familiar with the common layout quicker.
